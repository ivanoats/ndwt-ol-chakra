# Lock & Dam Protocol

The Portland District navigation locks on the Columbia and Snake Rivers
are operated by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. The Portland District
locks are:

- **Bonneville Dam** — Columbia River Mile 145
- **The Dalles Dam** — Columbia River Mile 191
- **John Day Dam** — Columbia River Mile 216

For up-to-date locking-through procedures and the current summer
recreational lockage schedule, contact the
[U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Portland District](https://www.nwp.usace.army.mil/).

## Non-motorized Boaters

Portage of non-motorized recreational craft and other vessels unsuitable
for lockage is the preferred method of transport around dams. Non-motorized
craft on the Columbia River may be locked through if moored to a motorized
assist vessel. On the Snake River, non-motorized craft may be locked
through without an assist vessel when no spill is occurring. For portage
routes around each dam, see the [portage guide](/river-navigation/portage-guide/).
